A Counselor's Lens

With over two decades as a school counselor, Rick’s writing is deeply rooted in empathy and human connection. His prose doesn't just describe a technical setting; it explores the psychology of the subject.

His style is characterized by nostalgic reflection, often bridging the gap between a modern technical process (like RAW editing) and a visceral, childhood memory. He writes with a "calm demeanor" that mirrors his approach to photography—putting the reader at ease while inviting them into a deeper conversation.

"Classic and contemporary, without being trendy."

Defining Traits

Narrative Technicality

He weaves technical specs (ISO, aperture, lens choices) seamlessly into personal anecdotes, making the gear feel like a character in the story.

Reflective Nostalgia

Frequent use of "The Season In-Between" motifs—exploring the passage of time and how memory shifts like a blurred background.

Self-Deprecating Honesty

Rick isn't afraid to discuss "Imposter Syndrome" or "regrettable purchases," building immediate trust with his audience through vulnerability.
