Mastering Color Correction Regions

Learn how to leverage the Color Toning masking tool in RawTherapee to gain surgical control over your edits.

1 The Foundation: Locating the Tool

The Color Correction Regions are housed inside the Color Tab (the palette icon) under the Color Toning module.

  • Enable Color Toning.
  • Switch the method to Color Correction Regions.
  • Click Add to create your first region.

[Interface Tip: Look for the 'Color Toning' drop-down at the bottom of the Color Tab]

2 The Precision Masking Workflow

The power of this tool lies in its ability to isolate specific tones using L*a*b* parameters. Follow this sequence for accurate results:

A. Visualize the Mask

Check the Show Mask box. By default, RawTherapee shows the mask in black and white (white is what will be affected). This is essential to ensure you aren't bleeding into unwanted areas like the sky or skin tones.

B. Use the L-Channel Equalizer

Most masking is best handled via the L (Luminance) equalizer. If you want to target only the shadows of a building, pull the right side of the curve down to zero. This excludes highlights from the selection.

C. Slope and Fall-off

Adjust the Slope and Offset sliders. The Slope controls how aggressively the mask picks up surrounding colors, while the Offset helps refine the black point of the mask.

Pro Tips for Accurate Results

Expand the Panel

Use the double-headed arrow to drag the Color Toning panel massively wide. This allows you to make extremely fine adjustments to the curves that are impossible in a small window.

Save Your Curves

Once you've crafted a perfect mask curve, click the Save Current Curve icon. You can reload this .rtc file in other modules or images to save hours of work.
