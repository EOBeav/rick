Mastering the Color Tab

The Color tab in RawTherapee is where the soul of your image is refined. It provides professional-grade tools to correct color casts, enhance mood, and isolate specific hues with surgical precision.

White Balance

The first step in any color workflow. This tool tells RawTherapee what "neutral white" looks like in your specific lighting conditions.

  • Method: Choose from Camera, Auto, or Manual presets (Daylight, Cloudy, etc.).
  • Temperature: Adjust the Blue-Yellow axis (Kelvin).
  • Tint: Fine-tune the Green-Magenta axis.
Pro Tip

Use the Spot White Balance (pipette tool) and click on a neutrally gray area of your image. For absolute precision, if you are in a difficult lighting situation, right-click the picker to adjust the sample area size (e.g., 16x16) to average out noise.

Vibrance & Saturation

While Saturation affects every pixel equally, Vibrance is "smart" saturation. It boosts muted colors more than already vibrant ones and includes built-in protection for skin tones.

Note: High Saturation settings can lead to "spray tan" effects on skin. Always prefer Vibrance for portraits.

Color Toning

Split Toning

Apply one color to the highlights and another to the shadows. Classic for the "Orange and Teal" cinematic look.

Masking Tool

Exclusive to RT 5.10+, use Hue, Chroma, or Luminance masks to restrict color toning to specific brightness levels.

Workflow Strategy

When using Color Toning, always check the Method dropdown. Methods like "L*a*b* Blend" allow you to add color without destroying the natural luminosity of the scene.

Local Adjustments (The Power Tool)

Local Adjustments in RawTherapee 5.10 are arguably the most powerful feature in the software. They function like "layers" in Photoshop but are based on DeltaE—a mathematical difference in color and light.

01

Add a Spot

Place a center point on the color you want to target (e.g., an eye or a leaf).

02

Scope Selection

The "Scope" slider determines how similar a color must be to the center point to be included in the mask.

03

Add Tool to Spot

Select "Color & Light" or "Tone Equalizer" to apply changes only to that specific region.

The "Magic Pop" Secret

To make a subject truly "pop," add a Local Adjustment spot to your subject, increase Chromaticity (in the L*a*b* adjustments panel), and slightly lift the Lightness. This creates separation without the "halo" artifacts often found in other software.

Ready to start processing?

RawTherapee is a complex beast, but the Color tab is where the magic happens. Experiment with the L*a*b* adjustments for the most natural-looking results.
