The Exposure Tool in RawTherapee

A comprehensive guide to managing luminance, tone curves, and highlight preservation.

Standard Exposure Controls

Exposure Compensation

The primary slider for shifting the image brightness. It works in EV (Exposure Value) steps, simulating a change in camera exposure.

Black Level

Sets the black point of the image. Increasing this makes shadows deeper and darker, helping to remove "haze" or lift contrast at the bottom of the histogram.

Shadows & Highlights

Separate from the dedicated "Shadows/Highlights" tool, these sliders in the Exposure tool allow for quick recovery of clipped areas or lifting of dark zones.

Saturation

A simple global adjustment for color intensity. Note that RawTherapee also offers Lab-based saturation in other panels for more natural results.

The Auto-Matched Tone Curve

Raw files are inherently "flat." Normally, a camera applies a proprietary tone curve to a JPEG to make it look pleasing. RawTherapee's Auto-Matched Tone Curve feature analyzes the embedded JPEG preview inside your Raw file (created by the camera) and attempts to match that look.

  • Purpose: Gives you a starting point that looks like the "camera-processed" image.
  • How it works: It automatically adjusts the Tone Curves (usually Tone Curve 1) to replicate the contrast and brightness of the camera's default profile.
  • Manual Tweak: Even after auto-matching, you can manually adjust the points on the curve to further refine the look.

Tone Curve 1 & 2

The Dual Curve Workflow

RawTherapee provides two curves. You can use Tone Curve 1 for global contrast (like an S-curve) and Tone Curve 2 for fine-tuning specific regions, or vice versa.

Standard: Classic control points.
Flexible: Uses splines for smoother transitions.
Parametric: Controlled by sliders (Highlights, Lights, Darks, Shadows).

Preserving & Reconstructing Highlights

Highlight Reconstruction Methods

Luminance Recovery

Recovers details in clipped channels using luminance data from non-clipped channels. Best for subtle recovery.

Color Propagation

The most powerful method. It "bleeds" color from surrounding unclipped pixels into the clipped areas. Excellent for skies and skin.

CIELab / Blend

Blends clipped areas to a neutral white or attempts to guess the color based on Lab space logic.

Pro Tip: Use the "Highlight Compression" slider in the Exposure tool to pull back "blown" highlights before they hit the tone curves.
