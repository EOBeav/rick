Dietary Wellness Blueprint

Personalized Nutritional Guidance for Renal and Metabolic Health

Clinical Summary

This plan is designed to manage high blood pressure, support liver health (fatty infiltration), and prevent the recurrence of kidney stones while addressing weight management (BMI 35.3).

✓ Foods to Include
  • Citrate-Rich Fruits Citrate helps prevent calcium from binding with other substances to form kidney stones.
    Lemonade, fresh lemons, oranges, melons
  • High-Phytate Whole Grains Phytates can help reduce the crystallization of minerals in the urine.
    Wheat, rice, beans, barley
  • Plant-Based Fibers High fiber intake supports weight management and improves liver health.
    Legumes, lentils, leafy greens, broccoli
  • Calcium-Rich Pairings Eating calcium with oxalate-rich meals helps minerals bind in the stomach instead of the kidneys.
    Low-fat yogurt, skim milk (consumed with meals)
✕ Foods to Avoid
  • High-Sodium (Salt) Products Sodium forces kidneys to excrete more calcium, increasing stone risk and raising blood pressure.
    Canned soups, deli meats, fast food, table salt
  • Excessive Animal Proteins Can increase uric acid levels and decrease citrate in urine, promoting stone formation.
    Red meat, shellfish, organ meats
  • Refined Sugars & Fructose Fructose increases stone-forming chemicals and worsens fatty liver conditions.
    Soft drinks, candy, high-fructose corn syrup
  • Saturated Fats Reducing these supports gallbladder health and manages cholesterol.
    Fried foods, butter, full-fat dairy, lard

Primary Goal: Critical Hydration

Drink at least 12 eight-ounce glasses (approx. 3 liters) of fluid daily, prioritizing water, to keep urine diluted.

Note: Sodium intake should be restricted to no more than 2,000 mg (1 teaspoon) per day. If using Vitamin C supplements, keep dosage under 1,000 mg daily.
