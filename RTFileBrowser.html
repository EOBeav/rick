The gateway to your raw workflow. Learn how to sort, filter, and batch process images with maximum efficiency.

Sorting & Filtering

Ranking System

Use keys 0-5 to rank images and +/- to color code them. This is the fastest way to "cull" a photoshoot directly in the browser.

Exif Filtering

The filter bar allows you to show only images taken with a specific lens, focal length, or ISO—perfect for analyzing your gear's performance.

Pro Tip:

Right-click the filter button to "Invert Filter." This allows you to quickly find images that don't match your current selection criteria.

The Inspect Tab (Key: F)

Located on the right side of the File Browser, the Inspect tab lets you peek into an image without opening it in the Editor.

  • 1. Instant Focus Check: Hover your mouse over the thumbnail while the Inspect tab is open to see a 100% crop of that area.
  • 2. RAW Data: It shows the raw histogram, which is much more accurate for checking clipped highlights than the processed JPG preview.
  • 3. Metadata: Quickly view full EXIF data including camera serial numbers, shutter count, and specific lens settings.

Batch Edit

You don't need to open every photo to apply changes. Select multiple images in the browser and use the right-hand panel:

Apply Profiles

Right-click > Processing Profile Operations > Apply. Sync dozens of photos to a "Standard" base profile in seconds.

Copy/Paste State

Ctrl+C on one image, select the rest, and Ctrl+V to paste the specific editing "DNA" across your set.

Fast Export

The "Fast Export" shortcut allows you to bypass the Queue tab for immediate results.

Select images and click the Hard Drive icon (Save) at the bottom.
Define your output (JPG/TIFF) and location in the popup.
Tip: If you use "Put to Queue," check the "Auto-start" box in the Queue tab to begin processing instantly.
