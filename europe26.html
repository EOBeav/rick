The Journey

Days 1 - 4

Budapest, Hungary

The "Pearl of the Danube" is where your adventure begins. Focus on the Jewish Quarter for architecture and ruin bars, but don't expect it to be "dirt cheap" anymore.

Day 1-2: Arrival & Welcome

Overnight flight. Welcome dinner with the Tour Director and group.

Day 3: Sightseeing the City

Hungarian State Opera House, Danube River views, and Matthias Church. Visit the Fisherman's Bastion.

Day 4: Free Time & Ruin Bars

Spend the full day in the Jewish Quarter. Tip: Avoid "Freelancer" taxis—use a cab app!

Days 5 - 7

Vienna, Austria

Imperial elegance meets coffee house culture. Remember: Shops are closed on Sundays!

Day 5: Arrival & Ringstrasse

Drive to Vienna via Bratislava. Dinner included with beer or wine.

Day 6: Imperial Sightseeing

Tour the Ringstrasse and Belvedere Palace. Keep 1€ coins ready for public toilets.

Day 7: Coffee Culture & Downtime

Free day to visit a traditional cafe. Tip: Don't stand on the left of escalators!

Days 8 - 11

Prague, Czech Rep.

The "City of a Hundred Spires." Watch out for currency scams on the street.

Day 8-9: Castles & Cobblestones

Prague Castle, St. Vitus Cathedral, and Old Town Square. Walk across Charles Bridge at sunrise to avoid crowds.

Day 10: Farewell Dinner

Celebrate the end of the main tour with your fellow travelers.

Day 11: Return or Extend

Transfer to the airport for your flight home, or continue to Salzburg!

Days 12 - 14

Salzburg, Austria

Extension Days! Sound of Music vibes, but locals probably haven't seen the movie.

Day 12: Salt Mines Excursion

Tour 450-year-old salt mines and take an underground boat on Mirror Lake.

Day 13: Bavarian Alps

Optional trip to the Eagle's Nest or Schnapps tasting in Berchtesgaden.

Day 14: Final Departure

Transfer to Munich for your final flight home.

Survival Guide

Financials

  • • No Street Exchanges: Never exchange money with guys on the street in Prague.
  • • Coin Stash: Always have 50c and €1 coins for public restrooms.
  • • Small Bills: Avoid €100/€200 bills; small shops won't take them.

Dining

  • • Water Hack: In Vienna, drink the tap water! It's world-class quality.
  • • Tipping: Round up or add 10%. In Austria, tell them the total you want to pay (don't leave cash on table).
  • • Bread: Ask before eating bread on the table; it's often not free.

Social Etiquette

  • • Escalators: Stand on the RIGHT, walk on the LEFT.
  • • Sundays: Grocery stores are CLOSED in Austria on Sundays.
  • • Jaywalking: It's illegal and strictly enforced in Austria. Wait for the green light!

Packing Note

Expect high heat in the summer (June/July). Many European hotels lack heavy AC. Pack a small day bag; museums often require you to check larger backpacks into lockers.
