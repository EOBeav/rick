The Power of HaldCLut

HaldCLut is a specialized technology used in RawTherapee to apply complex color transformations. Unlike a simple slider that changes "Saturation" or "Contrast" globally, a HaldCLut (Color Look-Up Table) tells the software: "Whenever you see this specific shade of blue, turn it into this specific shade of teal."

This allows for precise Film Simulations—emulating the exact color response, grain characteristics, and tonality of classic stocks like Kodak Portra 400 or Fuji Velvia.

Setting Up the Library

To use HaldCLuts in RawTherapee, you first need a library of .png CLut files. You don't apply them one by one; you point the software to a folder.

  1. Open Preferences (the cog icon at the bottom left).
  2. Go to the Image Processing tab.
  3. Under Film Simulation, locate the HaldCLUT directory field.
  4. Select the folder where you have stored your .png LUT files.

Once set, navigate to the Color Tab (the palette icon) in the editor, expand Film Simulation, and you will see your entire library in the dropdown menu.

RawTherapee treats the directory recursively. You can organize your LUTs into subfolders like "B&W", "Vintage", and "Cinematic", and they will appear neatly categorized in the dropdown menu.

Why Use HaldCLut Over Presets?

Presets (.pp3 files) simply move existing sliders. If a preset moves "Exposure" to +1.0, you've lost that room for adjustment. HaldCLuts are Internal Transformations. They happen within the processing pipeline, leaving all your sliders at zero so you can still tweak the image on top of the simulation.

Pro Tips for Professional Results

1. The "Amount" Slider is Your Best Friend

Most Film Simulations are designed for perfectly exposed images. If the effect feels too heavy, don't delete it—use the Strength slider. Setting it to 50-70% often yields a more "modern-pro" look than 100%.

2. Fix the Gamma First

Many CLuts are designed for a linear workspace. If your image looks "washed out" or "crushed" after applying a LUT, try changing the Input Profile under the Color Management tab, or adjust the Gamma slider within the Film Simulation tool itself.

3. Use the L*a*b* Adjustments in Tandem

HaldCLuts handle the "color recipe," but for the "pop," nothing beats the L*a*b* panel. Apply your LUT first, then go to the Exposure Tab > L*a*b* Adjustments to fine-tune the Chromaticity (saturation) and Lightness curves.

The Technical Edge

RawTherapee supports high-precision HaldCLuts. Standard 8-bit LUTs can cause "banding" or "posterization" in smooth gradients like skies. Always look for Level 12 or Level 16 HaldCLuts for the highest fidelity. These files are larger, but they ensure your 16-bit raw data isn't degraded during the color shift.
